## Overview

This page highlights Chanique Evrs' platform as a Miss Unity Netherlands 2026 competitor. The focus is on community, compassion, and personal empowerment — bringing attention to quality cancer support, survivor care, and programs that foster mental, emotional and physical wellbeing.

## About Chanique's Platform

Chanique believes that pageantry can be a powerful platform for change. Beyond the stage, her mission is to:

- Elevate access to compassionate cancer support services for patients and families.
- Promote holistic personal development programs that combine mental health, physical wellbeing and life-skills training.
- Partner with local care providers, survivor networks and youth programs to create sustainable initiatives.

## Programs & Initiatives

Chanique's current projects include:

- "Comfort & Care" — a volunteer-led support network offering practical assistance and emotional peer support to families affected by cancer.
- "Thrive Workshops" — community workshops focused on self-care, confidence-building and career guidance for young women.
- Fundraising talks and online campaigns to raise awareness and resources for local cancer support centers.

## How to Support

If you'd like to collaborate, volunteer, or support an initiative, please reach out via the contact details on the About page, subscribe to the newsletter, or join an upcoming event. Small partnerships and local sponsorships make these programs possible.

## Message from Chanique

"I stand for unity, resilience and care — and I believe every person deserves access to support that heals the whole person. Together we can create spaces where survivors and families find strength, dignity and hope."

## Advocacy & mission

Chanique is not only a design and engineering professional — she is a competitor at Miss Unity Netherlands 2026 and an active advocate for improved cancer support and complete personal development. She believes creative work can be a platform for social impact. If you'd like to collaborate on community events, survivor support programs, or wellbeing workshops, please reach out via the contact details on the about page.
